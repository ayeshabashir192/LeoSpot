Chargebacks

A chargeback is a refund processed by a card issuer for a merchant, on behalf of a cardholder. Chargebacks may be initiated by cardholders for various reasons, including unauthorized or fraudulent transactions, merchandise not received, or dissatisfaction with merchandise or services. If a chargeback is processed, the card issuer will debit the corresponding amount from the merchant's account and credit the cardholder's account.

Avoid currency fees

Many credit cards charge a fee for foreign transactions, and ATM withdrawals can also incur a fee. To avoid these fees, you can use a prepaid card. These cards can be loaded with local currency and can be used to make purchases and withdrawals without any fees.

Make payments globally

There are a number of ways to make payments globally. One way is to use a credit card. Credit cards can be used to make purchases anywhere in the world that accepts credit cards. Another way to make payments globally is to use a wire transfer. Wire transfers can be used to send money to anyone in the world with a bank account.
